Meet the Coral Crew: Ocean Architects of the Underwater World!

What are Corals ?

Corals are tiny marine animals called polyps that live in colonies and form massive structures over time. They belong to a group called Cnidarians, which also includes jellyfish and sea anemones. Despite looking like colorful rocks or plants, corals are actually living organisms!

Reef Builders of the Ocean

Hard corals, also called stony corals, build coral reefs by secreting calcium carbonate — the same material found in seashells. Over thousands of years, these structures form reefs, which serve as underwater cities for countless marine creatures.

Are Corals in danger ?

Yes. Many coral species are endangered due to climate change, pollution, overfishing, and ocean acidification. Rising sea temperatures cause coral bleaching — a condition where corals expel their algae and turn white, leaving them weak and vulnerable.

What can we do?

We can help corals by reducing plastic use, choosing reef-safe sunscreen, supporting marine conservation, and spreading awareness about the importance of protecting coral reefs. Small actions on land can lead to big impacts in the ocean!
